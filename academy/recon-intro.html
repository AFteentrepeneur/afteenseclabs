Module 1: Reconnaissance & OSINT

Before any attack begins, smart hackers do one thing first...

They Recon.

🔍 1. Core Concept – What is Reconnaissance?

Reconnaissance is the **first and most important phase** of any cyber attack. It means gathering as much information as possible about the target **without touching their systems**.

Think of it like a detective studying a house before breaking in. The better the reconnaissance, the easier and safer the attack becomes.

Memory Cue: "R.E.C.O.N" = Research Everything Carefully Or Nothing works.

🎯 2. Why Recon Matters

80% of Success
Good reconnaissance determines most of an attack’s success rate.
Low Risk
Passive recon is usually legal and very hard to detect.

Most companies are not hacked because of fancy tools. They are hacked because attackers knew exactly where to look.

🛠️ 3. Skills You Will Master

🌐

OSINT (Open Source Intelligence)

Gathering information from public sources like search engines, social media, and company websites.

📍

Domain & Subdomain Enumeration

Finding all domains and hidden subdomains (dev, admin, api, etc.).

📄

Metadata Extraction

Pulling hidden data from PDFs and documents (usernames, software versions, etc.).

👥

Social Media Intelligence

Analyzing employee posts to discover internal tools and technologies.

🔧 4. Tools You Will Learn

Maltego
Graph-based tool for mapping relationships and discovering domains.
Recon-ng
Powerful framework for automated OSINT.
theHarvester
Finds emails, subdomains, and hosts from public sources.
Amass
Advanced tool for discovering hidden subdomains.

📖 5. Real-World Example

A famous breach started with simple reconnaissance. The attacker found vpn.company.com using public tools. From LinkedIn, they identified IT staff. They then sent targeted phishing emails. This led to initial access and eventually a full network breach.

Memory Cue: "No recon = No direction. Good recon = Easy victory."
