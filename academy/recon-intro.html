"They Recon."

🔍 1. Core Concept — What is Reconnaissance?

Reconnaissance is the **information gathering phase** of a cyber attack. Attackers collect data about a target **without touching their systems**.

This phase is silent, legal (when done ethically), and extremely powerful.

Memory Cue: "R.E.C.O.N" = Research Everything Carefully Or Nothing works.

🎯 2. Why Recon Matters

📊

80% of Success

Good reconnaissance determines most of an attack’s success rate.

🛡️

Low Risk

Passive recon is usually legal and very hard to detect.

Most companies are not hacked because of fancy exploits.
They are hacked because attackers knew exactly where to look.

🧩 3. The 5 Intelligence Pillars

1. Assets
What exists? Domains, servers, applications.
2. People
Who operates it? Employees, admins, developers.
3. Technology
What powers it? Frameworks, cloud providers.
4. Exposure
What is visible? Public endpoints, open ports.
5. Weakness Indicators
Where are cracks forming? Outdated systems, misconfigurations.

🛠️ 4. Core Skills You Will Master

🌐

OSINT (Open Source Intelligence)

Gathering intelligence from public sources — search engines, social media, documents.

📍

Domain & Subdomain Enumeration

Finding main domains and hidden subdomains like dev, admin, api, vpn.

📄

Metadata Extraction

Pulling hidden data from PDFs — authors, software, internal paths.

👥

Social Media Intelligence

Analyzing employee posts to discover tools, projects, and internal systems.

📖 5. Real-World Attack Chain

A famous breach started with simple recon. The attacker found vpn.company.com.
From LinkedIn, they identified IT staff. They sent targeted phishing emails.
This led to initial access and eventually a full network breach.

Memory Cue: "No recon = No direction. Good recon = Easy victory."
