Module 1: Reconnaissance & Intelligence Gathering

🧠 “Information Wins Wars Before They Start.”

🎬 SECTION 0 — IMMERSIVE OPENING

You are not a hacker yet.
You are invisible. Unknown. Watching.

A company exists.
They have: Employees, Servers, Emails, Systems, Weaknesses.

They believe they are secure.
You haven’t touched their system.
Yet… you already know: Their internal tools, Their employee structure, Their exposed endpoints, Their likely entry point.

💥 This is reconnaissance.
The phase where attackers win before the attack even begins.

🎯 SECTION 1 — REFRAMING RECON

Most beginners think: “Recon is just gathering information.”
❌ Wrong.

🧠 Real Definition:
Reconnaissance is strategic intelligence acquisition for precision exploitation.

🔥 What You Are Actually Building:

  • A target profile
  • An attack blueprint
  • A decision advantage

⚠️ Brutal Reality: 80% of successful breaches are predictable — because recon exposed everything.

🧭 SECTION 2 — THE ATTACKER’S INTELLIGENCE FRAMEWORK

Elite attackers don’t “look around.” They operate with a structured model.

The 5 Intelligence Pillars
1. Assets
What exists? Domains, Servers, Applications
2. People
Who operates it? Employees, Admins, Developers
3. Technology
What powers it? Frameworks, Cloud providers, Software stacks
4. Exposure
What is visible? Public endpoints, Open ports, Leaked data
5. Weakness Indicators
Where are cracks forming? Outdated systems, Misconfigurations, Human mistakes

💡 This is how professionals think. Not randomly. Not emotionally. Systematically.

🟢 SECTION 3 — PASSIVE RECON (Silent Dominance)

Extract maximum intelligence without alerting the target.
🧬 Mindset Shift: You are not “searching.” You are investigating patterns.

🌐 Search Engine Intelligence (Google Dorking)
Examples:
site:target.com filetype:pdf
intitle:"index of" "backup"
Finds: Hidden directories, Exposed files, Sensitive documents.
📱 Social Media Profiling
Extract: Email formats, Internal tools, Project names, Organizational structure.
Example: “Excited to deploy our new AWS Kubernetes cluster!” → Cloud = AWS, Infrastructure = Kubernetes.
📄 Document Intelligence
Attackers extract: Authors, Software used, Internal naming conventions.
This feeds username discovery, password guessing, system identification.

🔴 SECTION 4 — ACTIVE RECON (Controlled Contact)

Carefully interact with systems to validate assumptions.
⚠️ Risk Layer: Logged, Detected, Blocked.
Activities: DNS queries, Subdomain probing, Service discovery.

Strategic Rule: Passive tells you what might exist. Active confirms what actually exists.

🌐 SECTION 5 — DOMAIN & INFRASTRUCTURE MAPPING

Step 1: Domain Expansion — Companies rarely operate one domain.
Example: target.com, target.net, target.org

Step 2: Subdomain Explosion — Realistic Structure: dev.target.com, staging.target.com, vpn.target.com, admin.target.com, api.target.com

💥 Most breaches start from: dev.target.com

🧑‍💼 SECTION 6 — HUMAN INTELLIGENCE (THE BIGGEST WEAKNESS)

Humans leak more than systems.
Example LinkedIn post: “Pushed new update using Jenkins on AWS!”
Intelligence Extracted: CI/CD = Jenkins, Cloud = AWS, Pipeline = Automated.

📄 SECTION 7 — METADATA: THE INVISIBLE LEAK

Files carry hidden intelligence.
Example: Report.pdf
Hidden Data: Author: admin-user, System: Windows 11, Software: Word 2019.
